About SignBridge

Conversation is a human right.

Around the world, 70 million people use a sign language as their first language. Yet most of the digital tools they touch every day — meetings, hospitals, classrooms, customer support — assume everyone speaks. SignBridge exists to close that gap.

What it does

SignBridge translates in both directions in real time. Speech becomes sign language animations on screen. Hand gestures captured by a webcam become spoken English audio. The two halves run side by side in Live Conversation mode so a Deaf person and a hearing person can actually talk — not pass notes, not wait for an interpreter.

How it works

For speech → sign, we tokenize and normalize tense with NLTK, then play sequenced sign animations from our video library. For sign → speech, MediaPipe Hands runs locally in the browser to track 21 joints per hand at 30+ fps; the live frames and landmarks are sent to Google Gemini 1.5 Flash Vision, which returns the English meaning of the gesture. The browser's SpeechSynthesis API speaks the result aloud. ElevenLabs voice integration is optional for richer, more expressive playback.

Why Healthcare Mode

Deaf patients face up to three times the rate of preventable medical errors, largely because they can't fully understand what their doctor is saying. In Healthcare Mode, every sentence the doctor says is run through Gemini first to strip out medical jargon and rewrite it in plain language — then it gets signed. The patient also leaves with a written transcript.
